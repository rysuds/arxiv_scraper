Executive Summary

Recent drug discovery papers on arXiv show a clear move from isolated molecule generation toward controllable, multi-step discovery pipelines. New methods combine diffusion or flow models with reinforcement learning, strategic search, and reaction-aware constraints to optimize multiple objectives at once. The latest work also improves practical reliability through governed agent architectures, sample-efficient optimization, and structure-aware design for protein-ligand systems. Overall, the field is progressing toward deployable, auditable AI systems for hit finding and lead optimization.

FuseDiff: Symmetry-Preserving Joint Diffusion for Dual-Target Structure-Based Drug Design
['Jianliang Wu', 'Anjie Qiao', 'Zhen Wang', 'Zhewei Wei', 'Sheng Chen']
http://arxiv.org/abs/2603.05567v1
FuseDiff tackles dual-target drug design by jointly generating one ligand and two target-specific binding poses in a single end-to-end diffusion model. Its dual-target local context fusion preserves shared molecular topology while allowing different geometries for each pocket. The paper reports state-of-the-art docking performance and introduces a more systematic way to assess dual-target pose quality before expensive pose search.
  • Jointly models ligand graph and both pocket-specific poses in one framework
  • Maintains symmetry and topological consistency across dual-target settings
  • Achieves state-of-the-art docking performance on benchmark evaluations
2026-03-04
Mozi: Governed Autonomy for Drug Discovery LLM Agents
['He Cao', 'Siyu Liu', 'Fan Zhang', 'Zijing Liu', 'Hao Li', 'Bin Feng', 'Shengyuan Bai', 'Leqing Chen', 'Kai Xie', 'Yu Li']
http://arxiv.org/abs/2603.03655v1
Mozi proposes a governed autonomy architecture for LLM agents in drug discovery pipelines, combining free-form reasoning with structured execution. The system separates control and workflow planes, enforces tool-use constraints, and adds human-in-the-loop checkpoints at high-uncertainty stages. On PharmaBench and case studies, it shows stronger orchestration reliability for long-horizon tasks such as candidate generation with safety filters.
  • Introduces supervisor-worker governance for safer agent tool usage
  • Adds stateful workflow graphs that map to canonical drug discovery stages
  • Demonstrates improved orchestration accuracy and robustness on PharmaBench

Designing the Haystack: Programmable Chemical Space for Generative Molecular Discovery
['Yuchen Zhu', 'Donghai Zhao', 'Yangyang Zhang', 'Yitong Li', 'Xiaorui Wang', 'Shuwang Li', 'Yue Kong', 'Beichen Zhang', 'Ricki Chen', 'Chang Liu', 'Xingcai Zhang', 'Tingjun Hou', 'Chang-Yu Hsieh']
http://arxiv.org/abs/2603.00614v1
This work reframes chemical space as a programmable object rather than a fixed latent distribution. SpaceGFN lets users define building blocks and reaction rules, then uses a GFlowNet to sample property-biased molecules within that designed space. Across 96 targets, it reports strong optimization under synthetic constraints and supports reaction-consistent editing for practical lead refinement.
  • Decouples chemical space design from exploration with programmable constraints
  • Uses evolution-inspired and pseudo-natural-product spaces to bias discovery
  • Shows broad optimization gains while retaining structural diversity

Toward Closed-loop Molecular Discovery via Language Model, Property Alignment and Strategic Search
['Junkai Ji', 'Zhangfan Yang', 'Dong Xu', 'Ruibin Bai', 'Jianqiang Li', 'Tingjun Hou', 'Zexuan Zhu']
http://arxiv.org/abs/2512.09566v2
Trio combines fragment language modeling, reinforcement learning, and Monte Carlo tree search to create a closed-loop molecular discovery process. The method balances novelty exploration with property-guided exploitation, instead of over-optimizing only affinity. Reported results include stronger binding affinity, drug-likeness, synthetic accessibility, and substantially improved diversity versus prior approaches.
  • Integrates LM generation, RL, and strategic search in one closed loop
  • Improves binding affinity (+7.85%), drug-likeness (+11.10%), and synthetic accessibility (+12.05%)
  • Expands molecular diversity by more than fourfold

FRAGMENTA: End-to-end Fragmentation-based Generative Model with Agentic Tuning for Drug Lead Optimization
['Yuto Suzuki', 'Paul Awolade', 'Daniel V. LaBarbera', 'Farnoush Banaei-Kashani']
http://arxiv.org/abs/2511.20510v2
FRAGMENTA introduces an end-to-end fragment-based generator that learns fragmentation as a vocabulary selection problem via dynamic Q-learning. It also adds agentic tuning where domain experts can iteratively refine objectives through natural language feedback. In cancer lead optimization experiments, the framework found nearly twice as many high-scoring molecules as baselines.
  • Replaces heuristic fragmentation with learned, optimization-aware fragmentation
  • Enables direct expert-in-the-loop objective tuning through an agent interface
  • Finds roughly 2x more high-scoring candidates in reported experiments
2025-11-12
SiDGen: Structure-informed Diffusion for Generative modeling of Ligands for Proteins
['Samyak Sanghvi', 'Nishant Ranjan', 'Tarak Karmakar']
http://arxiv.org/abs/2511.09529v3
SiDGen addresses the tradeoff between rich pocket conditioning and computational cost in structure-based design. It introduces a topological information bottleneck that compresses residue-level representations before pairwise reasoning, reducing memory while preserving binding-relevant structure. The paper reports state-of-the-art results on CrossDocked2020 and DUD-E with improved efficiency.
  • Uses a learned bottleneck to reduce pairwise complexity from full-scale pocket modeling
  • Preserves pocket-aware fidelity while improving memory and compute efficiency
  • Achieves state-of-the-art benchmark performance for ligand generation

Refine Drugs, Don't Complete Them: Uniform-Source Discrete Flows for Fragment-Based Drug Discovery
['Benno Kaech', 'Luis Wyss', 'Karsten Borgwardt', 'Gianvito Grasso']
http://arxiv.org/abs/2509.26405v1
InVirtuoGen proposes a discrete flow model that shifts molecular generation from masked completion to iterative refinement. By predicting all positions at each denoising step, it decouples generation behavior from strict sequence-length constraints and improves quality-diversity tradeoffs. It reports state-of-the-art practical molecular optimization results and stronger docking-oriented lead optimization outcomes.
  • Introduces refinement-first discrete flow generation for fragmented SMILES
  • Establishes a stronger quality-diversity frontier in de novo generation
  • Sets a new state of the art on practical molecular optimization benchmarks
2025-09-26
POLO: Preference-Guided Multi-Turn Reinforcement Learning for Lead Optimization
['Ziqing Wang', 'Yibo Wen', 'William Pattie', 'Xiao Luo', 'Weimin Wu', 'Jerry Yao-Chieh Hu', 'Abhishek Pandey', 'Han Liu', 'Kaize Ding']
http://arxiv.org/abs/2509.21737v1
POLO improves sample efficiency in lead optimization by training on full optimization trajectories instead of isolated single-turn edits. Its Preference-Guided Policy Optimization objective combines trajectory-level rewards with turn-level preference ranking signals. The paper reports strong gains, including 84% success on single-property tasks and 50% on multi-property tasks with only 500 oracle calls.
  • Learns from complete optimization trajectories for better chemical search behavior
  • Combines dense turn-level preferences with trajectory-level reinforcement signals
  • Delivers large sample-efficiency gains under tight oracle-evaluation budgets

DMol: A Highly Efficient and Chemical Motif-Preserving Molecule Generation Platform
['Peizhi Niu', 'Yu-Hsiang Wang', 'Vishal Rana', 'Chetan Rupakheti', 'Abhishek Pandey', 'Olgica Milenkovic']
http://arxiv.org/abs/2504.06312v3
DMol presents a graph diffusion platform designed for faster and more valid small-molecule generation. Compared to prior baselines, it improves validity, cuts required diffusion steps by at least 10x, and reduces runtime by around half. A compressed motif-aware variant further boosts validity and novelty while simplifying generation over ring-rich chemistry.
  • Improves validity over strong graph diffusion baselines
  • Reduces diffusion steps by at least 10x with major runtime savings
  • Motif-compressed variant further improves validity and novelty

GenMol: A Drug Discovery Generalist with Discrete Diffusion
['Seul Lee', 'Karsten Kreis', 'Srimukh Prasad Veccham', 'Meng Liu', 'Danny Reidenbach', 'Yuxing Peng', 'Saee Paliwal', 'Weili Nie', 'Arash Vahdat']
http://arxiv.org/abs/2501.06158v3
GenMol is a single discrete diffusion model that supports multiple drug discovery tasks, including de novo generation, fragment-constrained generation, hit generation, and lead optimization. It uses fragment-level SAFE sequences and remasking to efficiently explore and optimize molecular structures. The work reports state-of-the-art task performance while providing a unified model design instead of task-specific pipelines.
  • Unifies multiple molecular design tasks in one diffusion framework
  • Uses fragment remasking and molecular context guidance for optimization
  • Achieves state-of-the-art results on goal-directed hit generation and lead optimization
