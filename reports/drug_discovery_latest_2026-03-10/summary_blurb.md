# Latest Drug Discovery Findings (arXiv)

Recent arXiv work in AI-driven drug discovery is shifting from single-step molecule generation to governed, closed-loop discovery systems that jointly optimize potency, drug-likeness, synthesizability, and safety constraints. The strongest trend is tighter integration of generative models with structured optimization (diffusion/flow/RL/search), including methods that are more sample-efficient and synthesis-aware. A second trend is moving from single-target workflows toward more realistic settings such as dual-target design, explicit reaction-constrained editing, and long-horizon agentic orchestration with guardrails for reliability.

Several standout results include stronger docking and lead optimization quality, significant speedups in graph diffusion generation, and improved multi-objective optimization under limited oracle budgets. Overall, the latest papers indicate the field is maturing from proof-of-concept generation to practical, controllable, and auditable discovery pipelines.
